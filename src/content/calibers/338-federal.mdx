## History

The .338 Federal was introduced in 2006 as a collaboration between Federal Cartridge Company and Sako. The concept was straightforward and appealing: take the proven .308 Winchester case and neck it up to accept .338-caliber bullets, creating a cartridge that could handle elk and large game from the same short-action, lightweight rifles that hunters already carried for deer. The cartridge was designed to fill the gap between standard deer cartridges like the .308 Winchester and the heavy-recoiling .338 Winchester Magnum, offering 80 percent of the magnum's performance with significantly less recoil, weight, and cost.

Federal and Sako launched the cartridge with considerable enthusiasm, with Sako chambering their Model 85 in the new round and Federal providing a range of factory ammunition. The timing, however, proved challenging. The .338 Federal arrived during a period when the market was increasingly focused on long-range shooting and flat-trajectory cartridges, and the concept of a short-range, heavy-hitting cartridge in a short action did not capture the broader imagination. The shooting press received it warmly, and experienced hunters recognized its merits, but the .338 Federal never achieved the commercial momentum needed to become a mainstream offering.

## Modern Usage

Despite its limited commercial success, the .338 Federal is an exceptionally practical hunting cartridge that deserves more attention than it receives. With 200-grain bonded bullets at 2,560 fps, it generates nearly 3,000 ft-lbs of muzzle energy — more than enough for elk, moose, and large bear at ranges inside 300 yards. The larger-diameter .338-caliber bullets create wider wound channels and penetrate more deeply than .30-caliber projectiles of similar weight, offering a genuine terminal performance advantage on heavy-bodied game.

The cartridge's greatest appeal is the lightweight, handy rifle it enables. Because it operates at standard .308-class pressures in a short-action footprint, rifles chambered in .338 Federal can weigh under seven pounds and still handle comfortably. This makes it an outstanding choice for mountain hunting, backcountry elk hunts, and any situation where carrying a heavy magnum rifle is impractical. Recoil is noticeably less than the .338 Winchester Magnum, allowing faster follow-up shots and better accuracy for shooters who are sensitive to heavy recoil. Factory ammunition is available from Federal in several configurations, and brass can be easily formed from .308 Winchester cases, making it accessible to handloaders. The .338 Federal remains one of the best-kept secrets in the hunting cartridge world for those who value effectiveness over fashion.
