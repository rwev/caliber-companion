## History

The 6.5 Creedmoor was developed in 2007 by Hornady senior ballistician Dave Emary and competitive shooter Dennis DeMille. The cartridge was designed from the ground up for precision rifle competition, specifically to address the shortcomings that shooters experienced with the .308 Winchester at distances beyond 600 yards. By building on the .30 TC case (itself derived from the .308 Winchester), Emary created a cartridge optimized for the high-BC 6.5mm bullets that competitive shooters favored but had previously only been able to use in longer-action cartridges like the 6.5x55mm Swedish and .260 Remington. The shorter, fatter case design allowed reliable feeding, consistent powder ignition, and the ability to seat long, heavy-for-caliber bullets without intruding excessively into the powder column.

Initially the 6.5 Creedmoor gained traction slowly, primarily among PRS (Precision Rifle Series) competitors who appreciated its superior wind-bucking ability and reduced recoil compared to the .308 Winchester. Word spread through shooting forums and competition results, and by the mid-2010s, the cartridge had reached a tipping point of popularity that drove virtually every major rifle and ammunition manufacturer to offer products chambered for it.

## Modern Usage

Today the 6.5 Creedmoor is one of the most popular rifle cartridges in America, rivaling the .308 Winchester in sales and surpassing it in the precision rifle segment. It dominates PRS and NRL competition circuits, where its combination of low recoil, flat trajectory, and resistance to wind drift gives shooters a measurable advantage at extended ranges. The hunting community has also embraced the cartridge enthusiastically — its moderate recoil makes it accessible to a wide range of shooters, while its terminal ballistics are more than adequate for deer, antelope, and elk at reasonable hunting distances. The U.S. military has evaluated the cartridge for designated marksman and sniper roles, recognizing its superior ballistic performance over the 7.62x51mm NATO at range. Factory ammunition is widely available from every major manufacturer, with options ranging from affordable practice loads to premium match and hunting projectiles.
