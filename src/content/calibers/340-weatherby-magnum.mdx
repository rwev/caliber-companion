## History

The .340 Weatherby Magnum was introduced in 1962, filling what Roy Weatherby recognized as a gap between his .300 Weatherby Magnum and the massive .378 Weatherby Magnum. The cartridge was created by necking down the .378 Weatherby Magnum case slightly to accept .338-caliber bullets, then trimming it to fit standard magnum-length actions. The result was a cartridge that could push 250-grain .338-caliber bullets to nearly 2,900 fps — significantly faster than the .338 Winchester Magnum introduced four years earlier and offering a meaningful ballistic advantage at extended ranges.

The .340 Weatherby Magnum quickly earned a devoted following among serious elk hunters and outfitters in the American West, where long shots across deep canyons and high mountain basins are common. The combination of heavy .338-caliber bullets with high velocity produced exceptional energy retention at distance, and the cartridge developed a reputation for decisive kills on large-bodied game at ranges where lesser cartridges struggled. It became one of the most respected cartridges in Weatherby's lineup, though it was always overshadowed commercially by the more popular .300 Weatherby Magnum.

## Modern Usage

Today the .340 Weatherby Magnum is considered by many experienced hunters to be the ideal cartridge for elk, moose, and other large North American game at extended ranges. With 250-grain Nosler Partition or Barnes TTSX bullets at nearly 2,900 fps, it delivers over 2,100 ft-lbs of energy at 500 yards — more than enough for clean kills on the largest North American game. The cartridge also serves effectively on African plains game, where its flat trajectory and heavy bullet performance are well suited to species like eland and kudu.

The .340 Weatherby Magnum is available primarily in Weatherby's Mark V and Vanguard rifle platforms, with limited custom rifle availability. Weatherby produces the primary ammunition lineup through their Select Plus series, featuring premium bullets from Barnes, Nosler, and others. The cartridge generates substantial recoil that demands proper shooting technique and practice, but hunters who master it are rewarded with one of the most effective long-range big game cartridges in existence. Despite competition from newer .338-caliber magnums including the .338 Lapua Magnum and .33 Nosler, the .340 Weatherby maintains its reputation among knowledgeable hunters as the benchmark for high-velocity .338 performance.
