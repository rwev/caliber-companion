## History

The .33 Nosler was introduced in 2016 as part of Nosler's growing family of proprietary cartridges built on their modified .404 Jeffery parent case. Following the success of the .26 Nosler, .28 Nosler, and .30 Nosler, the company extended the lineup to .338 caliber to address the demand for a high-performance elk and large game cartridge that could fit in a standard-length bolt action. The .33 Nosler was designed to match or exceed the ballistic performance of the .340 Weatherby Magnum — itself one of the most powerful .338-caliber cartridges — while fitting into rifles built on the .30-06-length action, specifically Nosler's own M48 platform.

The engineering was accomplished through an efficient, large-capacity beltless case with a 35-degree shoulder angle and minimal body taper. This design maximized powder capacity within the 3.340-inch overall length constraint, allowing the .33 Nosler to push a 225-grain AccuBond bullet to over 3,000 fps and a 265-grain AccuBond Long Range to nearly 2,800 fps. SAAMI accepted the cartridge in 2016, and Nosler produced both rifles and ammunition from launch, establishing the .33 Nosler as a legitimate commercial cartridge rather than a wildcat.

## Modern Usage

The .33 Nosler has earned a devoted following among serious big-game hunters who want maximum .338-caliber performance without the weight penalty of a magnum-length action rifle. The cartridge excels on elk, moose, and other large-bodied North American game at ranges from close encounters in timber to 600-yard shots across mountain basins. With 225-grain bonded or monolithic bullets at over 3,000 fps, it delivers decisive terminal performance that inspires confidence even on the toughest animals.

The cartridge has also gained recognition for extended-range hunting applications, particularly when loaded with high-BC bullets like the 265-grain AccuBond Long Range. This combination produces a flat trajectory with excellent energy retention, keeping over 2,400 ft-lbs of energy at 500 yards. Rifle availability has expanded beyond Nosler's M48 to include offerings from Christensen Arms, Browning, and others. Ammunition is produced primarily by Nosler in their Trophy Grade line, with components available for handloaders. While the .33 Nosler competes in a crowded field of .338-caliber magnums, its standard-action compatibility and modern case design have secured it a place among the top choices for hunters seeking the most from the .338 bore diameter.
