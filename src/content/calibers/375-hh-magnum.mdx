## History

The .375 Holland & Holland Magnum was introduced in 1912 by the legendary British gunmaker Holland & Holland, making it one of the oldest magnum rifle cartridges still in widespread use. It was designed as a versatile medium-bore cartridge capable of handling any game on earth, from deer and plains game to the most dangerous animals in Africa and Asia. The cartridge featured an innovative belted case design — the first of its kind — which provided positive headspacing in the generous chambers typical of British bolt-action rifles of the era. This belted case would go on to become the parent case for virtually every commercial magnum cartridge developed over the next half-century.

The .375 H&H earned its legendary reputation in the hands of professional hunters and ivory hunters in East and Southern Africa during the colonial era. Hunters like Harry Selby, Robert Ruark, and Jack O'Connor praised it as perhaps the most versatile rifle cartridge ever designed. Its ability to handle everything from impala to elephant with appropriate bullet selection, combined with a surprisingly manageable recoil impulse for its power level, made it the overwhelming favorite of experienced African hunters. When various African nations established minimum caliber requirements for hunting dangerous game, the .375 bore was almost universally chosen as the threshold, effectively codifying the .375 H&H's status as the baseline dangerous game cartridge.

## Modern Usage

More than a century after its introduction, the .375 H&H Magnum remains the single most important cartridge in African dangerous game hunting. It is the minimum legal caliber for hunting elephant, cape buffalo, hippopotamus, and rhinoceros in most African countries, and it is the cartridge that professional hunters and guides across the continent most often carry as their backup rifle. Modern premium bullets have only enhanced its effectiveness — controlled-expansion designs like the Swift A-Frame, Nosler Partition, and Federal Trophy Bonded Bear Claw provide reliable penetration through the heaviest bone and muscle, while lighter expanding bullets in the 235 to 270-grain range make it an excellent choice for plains game.

Beyond Africa, the .375 H&H is highly regarded for hunting brown bears and moose in Alaska and northern Canada, where its stopping power provides a critical margin of safety in close encounters. The cartridge's relatively long, gently tapered case feeds exceptionally smoothly through bolt-action rifles, a characteristic highly valued when a fast follow-up shot may mean the difference between life and death. While numerous newer .375-caliber cartridges have been introduced over the years, none have come close to displacing the original. Its combination of proven performance, global ammunition availability, and over a century of real-world experience on the most dangerous game animals on earth ensures the .375 H&H Magnum will remain relevant for generations to come.
