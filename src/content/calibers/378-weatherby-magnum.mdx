## History

The .378 Weatherby Magnum was introduced in 1953, designed by Roy Weatherby to be the most powerful .375-caliber cartridge in existence. Rather than basing it on an existing belted magnum case like his earlier designs, Weatherby developed an entirely new, larger-diameter case with a 0.579-inch rim and a 2.913-inch case length. This massive case held significantly more powder than the .375 H&H Magnum, allowing the .378 Weatherby to push a 300-grain bullet at 2,925 fps compared to the H&H's 2,530 fps — a substantial velocity increase that translated into dramatically higher energy levels.

The cartridge required Weatherby's Mark V action, which was specifically designed to handle the larger case dimensions and higher pressures. The .378 Weatherby Magnum became the foundation for a family of cartridges, serving as the parent case for the .340 Weatherby Magnum, .416 Weatherby Magnum, and the mighty .460 Weatherby Magnum. While it never achieved the commercial success of the .375 H&H, the .378 Weatherby earned respect among a select group of professional hunters and dangerous game enthusiasts who valued its exceptional long-range energy retention on heavy game.

## Modern Usage

The .378 Weatherby Magnum occupies a unique niche as a long-range dangerous game cartridge. While the .375 H&H is generally considered adequate for all African dangerous game, the .378 Weatherby delivers significantly higher energy at all ranges, making it a more decisive stopper on Cape buffalo and a more forgiving cartridge when shots on dangerous game are taken at extended distances. Its flat trajectory for a .375-caliber cartridge also makes it effective on large plains game species like eland at ranges where the .375 H&H begins to lose effectiveness.

The cartridge is available exclusively in Weatherby's Mark V rifle platform, including the Dangerous Game Rifle model. Weatherby produces ammunition through their Select Plus line with bullets from Barnes, Hornady, and others. The .378 Weatherby generates extreme recoil that exceeds most shooters' comfort levels without significant practice and proper technique. A quality muzzle brake is nearly essential for regular shooting. Its rarity, expense, and punishing recoil limit its audience to dedicated dangerous game hunters, but for those who can manage it, the .378 Weatherby Magnum delivers a combination of .375-caliber versatility and exceptional power that no other production cartridge can match.
