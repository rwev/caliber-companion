## History

The .308 Winchester traces its origins to the U.S. military's post-World War II quest for a shorter, more efficient battle rifle cartridge. Through a series of experimental rounds designated T65, Army engineers at Frankford Arsenal shortened the .30-06 Springfield case from 2.494 inches to 2.015 inches while maintaining comparable ballistic performance with modern propellants. Winchester commercially introduced the cartridge in 1952 for their Model 70 and Model 88 rifles, two years before NATO formally adopted the military variant as the 7.62×51mm in 1954. The .308 and 7.62 NATO are often used interchangeably, though the military specification allows slightly lower chamber pressures than SAAMI-spec .308 Winchester.

The cartridge saw its first major combat use during the Vietnam War in the M14 rifle and M60 machine gun. It quickly proved itself as an outstanding sniper cartridge, with the U.S. Marine Corps adopting it in the M40 rifle system — a platform that remains in service in updated forms today. The Army followed with the M24 Sniper Weapon System, and the 7.62 NATO became the standard feed for Western general-purpose machine guns like the M240 and FN MAG.

## Modern Usage

Today the .308 Winchester occupies a unique position as perhaps the most versatile centerfire rifle cartridge available. In the hunting world, it is effective on game from whitetail deer to elk and moose at moderate ranges, with a vast selection of bullet weights and designs from 125-grain varmint tips to 180-grain controlled-expansion bonded projectiles. Its moderate recoil compared to magnum cartridges makes it accessible to a wide range of shooters, contributing to its enduring popularity in bolt-action hunting rifles like the Remington 700 and Savage 110 families.

In precision shooting, the .308 remains the standard entry point for long-range competition and is the dominant cartridge in law enforcement sniper roles. The Federal Gold Medal Match loaded with the 168-grain Sierra MatchKing became legendary for its accuracy, and the cartridge consistently delivers sub-MOA performance in quality rifles. While newer 6.5mm cartridges have challenged its dominance in competition at extreme distances, the .308's unmatched ammunition availability, barrel life, and real-world track record ensure it will remain a cornerstone of the rifle world for decades to come.
