## History

The .300 Precision Rifle Cartridge was introduced by Hornady in 2018, designed by a team led by ballistician Dave Emary. Unlike most magnum cartridges that evolved from belted magnum lineage, the .300 PRC was built on the non-belted .375 Ruger case, shortened and necked down to .308 caliber. The design goals were specific and deliberate: create a cartridge optimized for heavy, high-ballistic-coefficient .30-caliber bullets in the 212 to 230-grain range, with a long enough cartridge overall length to seat these bullets without intruding into the powder column, and with consistent, match-grade accuracy from the factory.

The cartridge arrived at a time when the long-range shooting community was pushing distances beyond what the .308 Winchester and even the 6.5 Creedmoor could efficiently reach. The .300 PRC filled the gap between the 6.5mm cartridges and the .338 Lapua Magnum, offering genuine 1,500-yard capability with manageable recoil. Its significance was validated when U.S. Special Operations Command selected the .300 PRC as the cartridge for the Advanced Sniper Rifle program, choosing it over the .300 Norma Magnum and other competitors. This military adoption cemented the cartridge's credibility and accelerated industry support.

## Modern Usage

The .300 PRC has rapidly established itself as one of the premier extreme long-range cartridges, earning a growing presence in ELR competitions and among serious long-range hunters. Its key advantage is that it was designed for the way modern precision shooters actually use heavy .30-caliber cartridges — with long, sleek, high-BC bullets seated to take full advantage of their potential. The Hornady 225-grain ELD Match load, with a G1 BC of approximately .777, retains supersonic velocity past 1,500 yards and delivers remarkable consistency for a factory cartridge.

For hunters, the .300 PRC offers a genuine step up over the .300 Winchester Magnum at extended ranges. The heavy bullets retain energy efficiently, arriving at 500 yards with more velocity and energy than most .300 Win Mag loads, while the modern bullet designs provide controlled expansion across a wider velocity window. The cartridge is particularly popular among western big-game hunters pursuing elk, mule deer, and sheep at distances where lesser cartridges become marginal. Rifle manufacturers have embraced the .300 PRC enthusiastically, with Christensen Arms, Bergara, Barrett, Browning, and Ruger all offering production models. While it requires a long-action or magnum-length receiver and demands respect for its recoil, the .300 PRC represents the current state of the art in .30-caliber precision cartridge design.
