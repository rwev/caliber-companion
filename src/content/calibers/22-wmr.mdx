## History

The .22 Winchester Magnum Rimfire was introduced by Winchester in 1959, making it the first new rimfire cartridge in decades and the most powerful rimfire commercially available at the time. Developed as a significant step up from the .22 Long Rifle, the .22 WMR used a longer case with a thicker wall and a full-diameter jacketed bullet, roughly doubling the muzzle energy of the .22 LR. The cartridge was initially chambered in Winchester's Model 61 pump-action and Model 255 lever-action rifles, and it quickly gained acceptance among farmers, ranchers, and small-game hunters who needed more reach and hitting power than the .22 LR could deliver.

The .22 WMR carved out a solid market position throughout the 1960s and 1970s as the go-to rimfire for serious pest control and small-game hunting. Its jacketed bullets provided better penetration and terminal performance at range compared to the lead or copper-plated bullets typical of .22 LR ammunition. The cartridge was eventually chambered in revolvers and semi-automatic handguns, further expanding its appeal. While the introduction of the .17 HMR in 2002 captured some of the varmint-hunting market, the .22 WMR retained loyal users who preferred its heavier bullets and superior penetration.

## Modern Usage

Today the .22 WMR occupies a well-established position as the utility rimfire, offering meaningful advantages over the .22 LR for shooters who need more capability without stepping up to centerfire ammunition and its associated cost and noise. For small-game hunters and trappers, the .22 WMR provides clean, humane kills on rabbits, squirrels, and raccoons at distances where the .22 LR becomes marginal. Ranchers and farmers rely on it for dispatching predators and pests, with effective range extending to roughly 125 yards on coyote-sized animals with proper shot placement.

The cartridge has found a renewed audience in the concealed-carry and personal-defense handgun market, with firearms like the Kel-Tec PMR-30 and Smith & Wesson Model 351PD offering high-capacity or lightweight options. While no rimfire cartridge is considered ideal for defensive use, the .22 WMR's jacketed hollow-point loads offer substantially more penetration and energy than the .22 LR. Ammunition remains widely available and affordable, produced by CCI, Winchester, Federal, Hornady, and others. The .22 WMR continues to serve as the practical workhorse of the rimfire world for those who demand more than the .22 LR can deliver.
