## History

The .257 Weatherby Magnum was developed by Roy Weatherby in 1944 as one of the original cartridges in his high-velocity magnum lineup. Weatherby, a passionate advocate for high velocity as the key to terminal effectiveness, created the cartridge by necking down his .300 Weatherby Magnum case to .257 caliber. The result was a cartridge that could push 100-grain bullets beyond 3,600 fps and 120-grain bullets past 3,300 fps — velocities that were nearly unheard of in the mid-1940s and remain remarkable today. Roy Weatherby himself publicly declared it his personal favorite cartridge, using it extensively on hunts around the world including African plains game.

The cartridge featured Weatherby's signature double-radius shoulder, a design element that Weatherby believed improved feeding reliability and promoted more complete powder combustion. While ballistic engineers have debated the actual benefits of this shoulder geometry, it became a trademark of the Weatherby magnum family. The .257 Weatherby Magnum was initially available only in Weatherby's premium Mark V rifles, which contributed to its reputation as an exclusive, high-end sporting cartridge. For decades it remained the undisputed velocity king of the .25-caliber class.

## Modern Usage

Today the .257 Weatherby Magnum continues to occupy a unique position as the ultimate long-range .25-caliber hunting cartridge. Its extraordinarily flat trajectory makes it a favorite among hunters who pursue pronghorn antelope, mule deer, and other open-country game at extended ranges where precise holdover estimation is critical. With a 100-grain bullet zeroed at 200 yards, the .257 Weatherby shoots remarkably flat to 400 yards, requiring minimal holdover for field shooting.

The cartridge is available in Weatherby's Mark V and Vanguard rifle lines, and a small number of other manufacturers offer it in custom or limited-production rifles. Weatherby produces the widest selection of factory ammunition through their Select Plus line, featuring premium bullets from Nosler, Barnes, and Hornady. Despite its impressive performance, the .257 Weatherby Magnum has remained a niche cartridge due to limited rifle availability, higher ammunition costs, and the relatively short barrel life that accompanies its extreme velocities. Nonetheless, for hunters who specialize in long-range open-country pursuits, it remains one of the most capable and exciting cartridges available.
