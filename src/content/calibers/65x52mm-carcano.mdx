## History

The 6.5×52mm Carcano was adopted by the Italian military in 1891 alongside the Carcano M91 bolt-action rifle, designed at the Royal Arms Factory in Turin. Italy was among the first nations to adopt a small-caliber smokeless-powder military cartridge, and the 6.5mm bore diameter offered excellent sectional density and moderate recoil — advantages that would be validated by the parallel development of similar 6.5mm cartridges in Sweden, Japan, and elsewhere. The round-nose 162-grain military bullet was not the most aerodynamically efficient, but it proved reliable across the varied terrain of Italy's military campaigns.

The cartridge saw extensive combat across both World Wars and Italy's colonial conflicts in Libya, Ethiopia, and East Africa. During World War I, Italian soldiers used the Carcano on the Alpine front against Austria-Hungary, where the rifle performed adequately in harsh mountain conditions. In World War II, the 6.5mm Carcano served alongside the newer 7.35×51mm Carcano, which Italy had attempted to adopt in 1938 but never fully fielded due to wartime production constraints. The 6.5×52mm is perhaps most widely recognized in American popular culture due to its association with the assassination of President John F. Kennedy in 1963, when Lee Harvey Oswald used a Carcano M91/38 carbine.

## Modern Usage

The 6.5×52mm Carcano occupies a niche position in the modern shooting world, kept alive primarily by collectors and surplus rifle enthusiasts. Millions of Carcano rifles were produced and many were imported to Western markets as affordable surplus firearms, though their unique 0.268-inch bullet diameter — larger than standard 6.5mm (.264) — complicates ammunition sourcing. Commercial ammunition is available from PPU and Hornady, with Hornady offering their 160-grain round-nose load specifically designed for the Carcano's bore dimensions. Handloaders must source the correct-diameter bullets, as standard 6.5mm (.264) projectiles are undersized for the Carcano bore. While not a popular hunting cartridge, the 6.5×52mm is fully capable on deer-sized game at moderate ranges, delivering performance comparable to the .30-30 Winchester.
