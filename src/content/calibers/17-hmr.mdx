## History

The .17 Hornady Magnum Rimfire was introduced in 2002 by Hornady Manufacturing in partnership with Marlin Firearms and Ruger. Created by necking down the .22 Winchester Magnum Rimfire case to accept a .17-caliber bullet, the .17 HMR was designed to be a quantum leap over the .22 LR and .22 WMR in terms of trajectory, accuracy, and effective range. At its debut, the 17-grain V-Max bullet at 2,550 fps offered dramatically flatter shooting than any rimfire cartridge before it, enabling confident shooting at varmints and small game beyond 150 yards.

The cartridge was an immediate commercial success, generating excitement not seen in the rimfire market in decades. Within months of its introduction, virtually every major rifle manufacturer offered bolt-action models in .17 HMR, and the round quickly became one of the best-selling rimfire cartridges in America. Savage's Model 93R17 became a benchmark for affordable .17 HMR accuracy. Early attempts to chamber semi-automatic rifles proved problematic due to occasional case ruptures from the thin brass, and the cartridge is generally recommended for bolt-action, lever-action, and single-shot platforms, though some semi-automatic designs have since been refined.

## Modern Usage

The .17 HMR has established itself as the premier rimfire varmint cartridge, filling the gap between the .22 LR and centerfire options like the .223 Remington. Its flat trajectory and explosive terminal effect on impact make it exceptionally effective on ground squirrels, prairie dogs, rabbits, and other small pests out to about 200 yards. The tiny .17-caliber bullet fragments rapidly, minimizing the risk of dangerous ricochets compared to heavier .22-caliber projectiles, which is a significant advantage when shooting near structures or livestock.

For predator hunters, the .17 HMR offers a quiet, low-recoil option for dispatching coyotes and foxes at closer ranges, though its light bullets limit effectiveness beyond 100 yards on animals of that size. The cartridge is also popular among recreational target shooters who appreciate its accuracy and minimal report. Ammunition remains widely available and affordable, with offerings from Hornady, CCI, Federal, and Winchester. The .17 HMR has permanently expanded the rimfire market segment and remains the cartridge of choice for shooters who want the greatest practical performance from a rimfire platform.
