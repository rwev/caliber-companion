## History

The .300 Norma Magnum was developed by the Swedish ammunition manufacturer Norma Projektilfabrik and introduced commercially in 2013. The cartridge was designed from the ground up for extreme long-range precision shooting, using a non-belted case based on the .338 Norma Magnum necked down to .308 caliber. This large-capacity case was specifically optimized to efficiently propel very heavy, high-ballistic-coefficient .30-caliber bullets in the 200-230 grain range at velocities exceeding 2,900 fps — a critical combination for maintaining supersonic flight and delivering energy at distances beyond 1,500 yards.

The cartridge's most significant endorsement came when it was selected by U.S. Special Operations Command (SOCOM) for the Advanced Sniper Rifle (ASR) program, which ultimately produced the MK22 Multi-Role Adaptive Design (MRAD) sniper rifle system built by Barrett Firearms. This selection validated the .300 Norma Magnum's superior ballistic performance over the established .300 Winchester Magnum, particularly at extreme ranges where the heavier, higher-BC bullets maintained a decisive advantage in wind resistance and energy retention. The Barrett MRAD system was designed to be convertible between .300 Norma Magnum, .338 Norma Magnum, and 7.62 NATO.

## Modern Usage

The .300 Norma Magnum has rapidly established itself as a premier extreme long-range cartridge for both military and competitive applications. Its adoption by U.S. SOCOM and deployment across special operations units has driven significant development in both rifle platforms and ammunition. The cartridge bridges the gap between the .300 Winchester Magnum and the .338 Lapua Magnum, offering substantially better long-range ballistics than the .300 Win Mag while producing less recoil than the .338 Lapua and fitting into a slightly more compact platform.

In the competitive long-range shooting community, the .300 Norma Magnum has gained traction in Extreme Long Range (ELR) matches, where targets may be engaged at 1,500 to 2,500 yards. The combination of heavy 215-230 grain bullets with very high ballistic coefficients produces exceptional wind resistance and energy retention at these distances. Rifle platforms include the Barrett MRAD, Accuracy International AX, and various custom precision rifle builds. Ammunition is available from Norma, Berger, and several specialty manufacturers. While the cartridge remains niche due to its specialized nature and the expense of appropriate rifle systems, its military adoption ensures continued development and growing availability in the years ahead.
