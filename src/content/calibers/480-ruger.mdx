## History

The .480 Ruger was introduced in 2003 as a joint development between Sturm, Ruger & Co. and Hornady Manufacturing. The cartridge was inspired by the .475 Linebaugh, a powerful wildcat cartridge developed by John Linebaugh in the 1980s that had earned a devoted following among handgun hunters. Rather than simply chamber the full-power .475 Linebaugh, Ruger and Hornady created a shorter, slightly reduced-pressure cartridge that would deliver substantial big-bore performance while keeping recoil within a more manageable range.

The .480 Ruger uses the same .475-inch diameter bullet as the .475 Linebaugh but in a case shortened from 1.400 inches to 1.285 inches. Operating at a maximum pressure of 48,000 psi, it generates approximately 20% less recoil than the .454 Casull while delivering comparable or greater energy with heavier bullets. Ruger introduced the cartridge in their Super Redhawk revolver, a platform already proven with the .44 Magnum and .454 Casull.

## Modern Usage

The .480 Ruger was designed with a specific philosophy: deliver the maximum practical big-bore performance that an experienced shooter can handle effectively in the field. Unlike the .500 S&W and .460 S&W that push the absolute limits of handgun power, the .480 Ruger aims to be a working cartridge that can be shot accurately under field conditions by hunters who have practiced with it.

With 325-grain bullets at 1,350 fps, the .480 Ruger is fully capable of taking any North American game, including moose and the great bears. Its large-diameter, heavy bullets provide deep penetration on tough, heavy-boned animals. The cartridge has a small but loyal following among serious handgun hunters who appreciate its blend of power and shootability. However, limited ammunition availability has been the cartridge's greatest challenge, with only a few manufacturers offering factory loads. Handloaders have access to a wider selection of .475-caliber bullets and can tailor loads to their specific needs, making reloading almost essential for dedicated .480 Ruger shooters.
