## History

The .460 Weatherby Magnum was introduced in 1958 and was specifically designed to be the most powerful shoulder-fired cartridge available. Roy Weatherby created it by necking up the .378 Weatherby Magnum case to accept .458-caliber bullets, then loading it to significantly higher velocities than any existing .45-caliber cartridge. The result was staggering: a 500-grain bullet at 2,600 fps, generating over 7,500 ft-lbs of muzzle energy. This represented a massive increase over the .458 Winchester Magnum's approximately 5,000 ft-lbs and even surpassed the legendary .600 Nitro Express in kinetic energy.

The cartridge was developed with one primary purpose — providing the ultimate stopping power for the world's most dangerous game animals, particularly African elephant and Cape buffalo. Its development was driven partly by stories from professional hunters who encountered situations where lesser cartridges failed to stop charging dangerous game. The .460 Weatherby Magnum was designed to eliminate any question of adequate power. For decades it held the undisputed title of the world's most powerful commercial rifle cartridge, a distinction it maintained until the introduction of the .700 Nitro Express and several large proprietary wildcats.

## Modern Usage

The .460 Weatherby Magnum remains the most powerful cartridge in Weatherby's lineup and one of the most powerful commercially available rifle cartridges in the world. It serves a very specialized role as the ultimate dangerous game stopper for hunters who want absolute maximum power for elephant, Cape buffalo, and hippopotamus. The cartridge delivers devastating terminal performance, with 500-grain solids capable of penetrating through the heaviest bone structure of any land animal.

Available exclusively in Weatherby's Mark V platform, including the Dangerous Game Rifle and Safari Custom models, the .460 Weatherby Magnum is a rare sight even in African hunting camps. Its recoil is legendary — approximately 100 ft-lbs of free recoil energy — and can cause injury to unprepared shooters. A heavy rifle, proper technique, and extensive practice are absolute prerequisites. Ammunition is expensive and produced in limited quantities by Weatherby, with bullets from Barnes, Hornady, and others. The .460 Weatherby Magnum is not a practical cartridge by any conventional measure, but for the small number of hunters who pursue the world's most dangerous game and demand the ultimate in stopping power, it remains the benchmark against which all other big-bore cartridges are measured.
