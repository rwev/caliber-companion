## History

The .30 Super Carry was introduced by Federal Ammunition in January 2022 as the first entirely new handgun cartridge from a major American manufacturer in decades. Federal's engineering team set out to answer a specific question: could they create a cartridge that matched the terminal performance of the 9mm Luger while fitting in a smaller-diameter package that would allow two or more additional rounds in a comparably sized magazine?

The answer was a .312-caliber cartridge operating at high pressure (45,000 PSI) to drive a 100-grain bullet to approximately 1,200 fps from a compact pistol barrel. Federal partnered with Smith & Wesson to launch the cartridge alongside the M&P Shield Plus chambered in .30 Super Carry, which offered a 16+1 capacity in a pistol frame that held only 13+1 rounds of 9mm. Nighthawk Custom also announced a 1911-pattern pistol in the new chambering.

## Modern Usage

The .30 Super Carry entered a challenging market as a new, unproven cartridge competing against the deeply entrenched 9mm Luger. Its core value proposition — two to three additional rounds of near-9mm performance in the same-sized handgun — is compelling in theory, but faces significant headwinds. Ammunition is expensive and available from limited sources, firearms options remain very limited, and the 9mm's enormous ecosystem of ammunition, firearms, holsters, and accessories represents a formidable incumbent advantage.

Early ballistic testing by independent reviewers showed that the .30 Super Carry does deliver on its performance claims, with Federal's HST load producing expansion and penetration in ballistic gelatin that is competitive with standard-pressure 9mm loads. However, market adoption has been slow, and the cartridge's long-term viability remains uncertain. Its success or failure will likely depend on whether additional firearms manufacturers embrace the caliber and whether ammunition prices decrease as production scales. For now, it remains a niche option for early adopters who prioritize maximum capacity in a compact carry pistol.
