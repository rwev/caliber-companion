## History

The 6.5×55mm Swedish was jointly developed by Sweden and Norway in 1894 through a combined Scandinavian committee seeking a modern smokeless-powder military cartridge. The result was a round of exceptional balance — the 6.5mm bore diameter provided excellent sectional density with long, streamlined bullets, while the generous case capacity delivered respectable velocities without excessive pressure or recoil. Sweden adopted it in the m/96 Mauser bolt-action rifle, while Norway chambered it in their Krag-Jorgensen rifles.

The cartridge served both Scandinavian nations faithfully through the first half of the 20th century. Though Sweden remained neutral during both World Wars, the Swedish military maintained large stockpiles of 6.5×55mm ammunition and continued to develop the cartridge, producing some of the finest military ammunition ever loaded. Swedish match-grade 6.5×55mm was legendary for its accuracy, and the combination of the Swedish Mauser action with the inherently accurate 6.5mm bore made the m/96 one of the most precise military rifles of its era.

## Modern Usage

The 6.5×55mm Swedish has enjoyed a remarkable renaissance in the modern era, particularly as the broader shooting world has rediscovered the virtues of 6.5mm cartridges. In Scandinavia, it remains the dominant hunting cartridge — Swedish and Norwegian hunters use it extensively for moose, reindeer, and deer, trusting its deep penetration and clean killing performance with heavy-for-caliber bullets. Internationally, competitive shooters prize it for long-range precision work, and the cartridge's inherent accuracy has made it a favorite in European benchrest and military-style competitions. Modern ammunition from Norma, Lapua, and other manufacturers takes full advantage of the cartridge's capabilities, and it is chambered in contemporary sporting rifles from Tikka, Sako, CZ, and others. The 6.5 Swede is often regarded as the cartridge that proved the superiority of 6.5mm bore diameters decades before the 6.5 Creedmoor brought that concept to mainstream American attention.
