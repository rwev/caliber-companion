## History

The 5.56×45mm NATO traces its origins to the late 1950s, when the U.S. military began exploring small-caliber, high-velocity rifle cartridges as an alternative to the heavy-recoiling 7.62×51mm NATO. Remington developed the .223 Remington in 1957 for ArmaLite's AR-15 rifle, and early testing demonstrated that the lightweight cartridge allowed soldiers to carry significantly more ammunition while delivering devastating wound ballistics at combat distances through its high velocity and tendency to fragment. Despite fierce institutional resistance from ordnance officials who favored the larger 7.62mm, Secretary of Defense Robert McNamara pushed adoption forward, and the cartridge entered U.S. service in 1964 alongside the M16 rifle during the Vietnam War.

The early years in Vietnam were troubled — the original M16 suffered from reliability issues related to a change in powder type and the military's initial failure to issue cleaning kits — but once these problems were resolved, the M16 and its 5.56mm cartridge proved effective. NATO formally standardized the 5.56×45mm in 1980, adopting the Belgian SS109 62-grain projectile (designated M855 in U.S. service) as the alliance standard. This cemented the cartridge's dominance across Western military forces and drove massive global production capacity.

## Modern Usage

Today the 5.56×45mm NATO is the most widely used military rifle cartridge in the world. The U.S. military fields it in the M4 Carbine, M16A4, and M249 SAW, while NATO allies chamber it in platforms ranging from the HK G36 to the FN F2000 and Steyr AUG. Continued development has produced improved projectiles like the M855A1 EPR (Enhanced Performance Round), which offers better performance against barriers and intermediate obstacles than its predecessor. On the civilian side, the cartridge's commercial equivalent — the .223 Remington — dominates the AR-15 platform, making it the most popular centerfire rifle cartridge in the United States. Its low recoil, flat trajectory, and wide ammunition selection make it equally suited to home defense, target shooting, varmint hunting, and competition use.
