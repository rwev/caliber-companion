## History

The 6.5 Grendel was developed in 2003 by Bill Alexander of Alexander Arms in collaboration with competitive shooters Arne Brennan and Janne Pohjoispaa. The cartridge was based on the .220 Russian case (which also spawned the 6mm PPC benchrest cartridge), necked up to 6.5mm and optimized for the AR-15 platform. The design philosophy was to create the most ballistically efficient cartridge possible within the length and magazine constraints of the AR-15 — using high-BC 6.5mm bullets to dramatically outperform the 5.56mm NATO at distances beyond 300 yards while still fitting in a standard AR-15 lower receiver with a modified bolt and magazine.

The Grendel represented a genuinely novel approach to AR-15 cartridge design. Rather than simply increasing bore diameter or case capacity, Alexander focused on ballistic coefficient and sectional density, understanding that a smaller, more aerodynamic bullet could outperform larger projectiles at distance despite lower muzzle velocity. Early testing demonstrated that the 6.5 Grendel could remain supersonic past 1,000 yards from a 24-inch barrel — a feat far beyond the capability of the 5.56mm NATO. However, trademark disputes between Alexander Arms and the broader firearms community initially hampered adoption until SAAMI standardized the cartridge in 2011.

## Modern Usage

The 6.5 Grendel has settled into a well-defined niche as one of the premier long-range AR-15 cartridges. It offers a significant step up from 5.56mm in both effective range and terminal performance on medium game, making it popular among deer and hog hunters who want to use the AR-15 platform. The cartridge's low recoil — comparable to the 5.56mm — makes it pleasant to shoot, and the inherent accuracy of the design supports excellent precision at extended ranges. Factory ammunition is available from Hornady, Wolf, Federal, and Alexander Arms, with options ranging from affordable steel-case practice loads to premium match and hunting projectiles. The cartridge faces increasing competition from newer designs like the 6mm ARC and .224 Valkyrie, but its established track record, proven terminal performance, and broad ammunition availability ensure it maintains a loyal following among AR-15 enthusiasts who prioritize long-range capability.
