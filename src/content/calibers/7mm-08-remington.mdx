## History

The 7mm-08 Remington began its life as a wildcat cartridge in the early 1960s, when metallic silhouette competitors and experimentally minded handloaders necked the .308 Winchester case down to accept 7mm (.284-caliber) bullets. The combination proved remarkably efficient — the 7mm bore diameter offered excellent bullet selection with high ballistic coefficients, while the .308 case provided enough powder capacity to drive those bullets at useful velocities. The wildcat gained a loyal following among competitive shooters and knowledgeable hunters throughout the 1960s and 1970s.

Remington officially standardized the cartridge in 1980, introducing it in their Model 788 bolt-action rifle and their Model 7600 pump-action. The timing was somewhat unfortunate, as the 7mm-08 entered a market already saturated with hunting cartridges, and Remington's marketing efforts were modest compared to what they had invested in cartridges like the 7mm Remington Magnum. Nevertheless, the 7mm-08 steadily built a reputation through word of mouth and the enthusiastic endorsement of handloaders and hunting writers who recognized its outstanding balance of performance, accuracy, and recoil.

## Modern Usage

The 7mm-08 Remington has grown into one of the most respected hunting cartridges in North America. Its appeal rests on a compelling combination: it generates roughly 85% of the energy of the .270 Winchester or .280 Remington while producing noticeably less recoil, and it fits in compact, lightweight short-action rifles that handle well in the field. With 140-grain premium bullets, the 7mm-08 is a thoroughly capable deer and elk cartridge out to 400 yards and beyond. It has become particularly popular as a youth and recoil-sensitive hunter's cartridge, since its moderate kick allows shooters to practice without developing a flinch while still delivering terminal performance well beyond what the .243 Winchester can offer. The cartridge also sees continued use in precision rifle competition, where its inherent accuracy and efficient case design reward careful handloading. Every major rifle manufacturer chambers at least one model in 7mm-08, and ammunition availability is excellent.
